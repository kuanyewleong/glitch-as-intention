Glitch as Intention:

The Wabi-Sabi of AI Art

Introduction

This gallery showcases the convergence of human creativity and machine intelligence. Each artwork is generated or enhanced through a synergy between deep learning algorithms and traditional artistic sensibilities.

The aim is to provoke thought about the boundaries of authorship, imagination, and the creative potential of artificial intelligence in the visual arts.

Conceptual Approach

The pieces presented here stem from experiments with style transfer, generative adversarial networks (GANs), diffusion models, and fine-tuned models trained on thematic datasets.

Various themes—from nature and surrealism to symmetry and abstraction—are explored through these works to reflect the diversity and depth of AI-generated creativity.

Methodology

Most works were generated using pretrained GANs such as StyleGAN2 and Diffusion-based frameworks like Stable Diffusion. Some were fine-tuned with custom prompts and curated image datasets.

Post-processing techniques were applied to balance textures, colors, and structural harmony—merging algorithmic precision with human aesthetic judgment.

About the Artist

Kuan Yew, LEONG is a specialist of Computer Vision and Deep Learning based in Kyoto, Japan. Currently, he works on the improvements of computer vision by researching different learning algorithms. Kuan Yew demonstrates a keen interest in the intersection of art, natural science, and technology through the design and development of sophisticated smart paludariums.

He maintains a forward-thinking perspective, recognizing that present-day challenges represent opportunities for innovation, and is committed to leveraging technological advancements for future betterment.
